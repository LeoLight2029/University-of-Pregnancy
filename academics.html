Curriculum

A course path that follows reproduction from preconception through childbirth.

View Program

Program Progression by Year

Freshman Year

CourseContent
What Can I Do Throughout? Focus on what to do throughout pregnancy. Moderate exercise such as walking or yoga as well as proper nutrition is essential.
Embryology 101 The embryo is the early developmental stage of a human that goes from fertilization to the 8th week of pregnancy. The single zygote goes through rapid cell division and develops basic anatomical structures.
1st Trimester During the 1st trimester, fertilization occurs when the sperms meet the ovum and create the zygote. The zygote then goes into the uterus for implantation and the embryo starts to develop. The Placenta (see departments) begins to form at the 4th week of pregnancy. This lasts from fertilization to the 12th week of pregnancy.

Sophomore Year

CourseContent
Fetal Development Building off the embryology course, the organism being developed is now a fetus. Organs are now growing with fascial features being distinct. The nervous system is also beginning to function as rapid growth continues.
Fetus The fetus is the unborn human at the 9th week of pregnancy. There are now recognizable human features and goes on until the 11th week of pregnancy.
2nd Trimester This trimester lasts from weeks 12-26 where the fetus continues to grow as it develops muscle control and has movement that is detectable for the mother. The accessory organs (skin, hair and nails) begin to form as internal organs also continue to develop.
What Shouldn't I Do?/Fetal Risks Avoiding harmful substances like alcohol, drugs or tobacco are also crucial. Be careful of environmental toxins, lack of sleep, or excessive stress.

Junior Year

CourseContent
3rd Trimester These are weeks 26-40 of pregnancy where the fetus gains weight rapidly. The internal organs are mature and movement becomes stronger. The fetus prepares for birth as it moves into a position where its head is faced downwards for easier birth.
Childbirth Prep This class should be taken concurrently with the 3rd Trimester. Individuals will learn about labor and delivery techniques which include pain management during the birthing process. Preparing mentally is just as important as physically.
Braxton Hicks Contractions Irregular contractions that happen periodically that prepares the body for true labor. They are mildly painful or uncomfortable and occur without cervical dilation.
Cervix Dilation The walls of the cervix begin to thin and dilate as it prepares for labor. Full dilation is usually around 10 cm wide which allows for the baby to pass through. Monitoring of dilations is crucial for labor progression.

Senior Year

CourseContent
Contractions Regular uterine contractions that help with labor by pushing the baby through the birth canal. Contractions increase in intensity, pain, and frequency as labor progresses.
Water Breaking Rupture of the amniotic sac releases fluid which signals labor is about to start and requires monitoring to ensure a successful birth.
