Labor & Birth Curriculum

Overview of Labor, Interventions & Postpartum

These are programs offered to our graduate students. The order these programs are taken are not negotiable and must me taken in the order they are presented below.

1. First Stage of Labor

1st Stage of Labor
This period starts with regular uterine contractions with the cervix fully dilated to 10 centimeters. This stage is the longest and has two phases: early labor and active labor. In early labor cervix dilates from 0 to 4-6 centimeters with irregular and mild contractions. In Active Labor the cervix fully dilates and contractions are stronger and more frequent.

2. Interventions During Labor (When Needed)

Pitocin
This is a synthetic medication that is derived from oxytocin that helps induce labor and other childbirth processes.
Epidural
This is a regional anesthetic that uses a catheter near the spine to deliver pain management medications.

3. Second Stage of Labor

2nd Stage of Labor
This stage begins when the cervix is fully dilated to 10cm and ends with the birth of the baby. The mother will experience powerful contractions with the natural urge to push. The contractions in this period are usually 45-90 seconds each with 3-5 minutes of break in between. Crowning is when the baby first emerges and it is normal for minor urination to occur.

4. Third Stage of Labor

3rd Stage of Labor
This is the period after the baby is born but before the placenta (see departments) is delivered. This lasts no more than 130 seconds and has few contractions to help deliver the placenta. The umbilical cord (see departments) is clamped after pulsation stops and is cut.

5. Postpartum Recovery

Breastfeeding
This is the process where the mother feeds the baby with milk that is produced in the breasts. Breastmilk naturally supports immunity and helps bonding between mother and infant.
Postpartum Depression
This is a mood disorder that occurs after childbirth that can affect individuals for up to a year. Symptoms include persistent sadness, fatigue, and loss of sexual activities.
